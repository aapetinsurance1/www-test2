One of the main reasons why it is difficult to choose the insurance is because many of the benefits are not quantitative. Yes, in the best case scenario, if your pet ended up being absolutely healthy, you would have saved money if you didn't buy pet insurance. In the worst case scenario, your pet is very ill and you save a lot of money because you did buy pet insurance. How much does it worth to you knowing that you can afford any veterinary care your pet might need in the future? It is difficult to put a price tag on worry and stress, even though it is well known that both shorten our lifespan.

It is difficult to put a price tag on worry and stress, but it is possible to figure out what is more important to you. Will paying higher premium give you more stress, or knowing that you have a deductible? In addition to monthly premiums comparisons, we are hoping to incorporate qualitative decision making science to help pet parents with pet insurance choices.

On that note, would it be appropriate to analyze pet parent's Facebook profile to understand their personality traits and recommend insurance that works best for people like them?
